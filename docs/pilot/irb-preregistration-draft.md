# IRB And Pre-Registration Draft

This document frames pilot measurement as a reviewable education improvement study.

## Study Question

Does an instructor-approved AI-generated bridge explanation reduce repeated struggle at a lecture segment and improve the next checkpoint result?

## Scenario

S01 Closed Loop Struggle Intervention.

## Population

One selected university course section during a 90-day pilot window.

## Intervention

An instructor-approved bridge explanation and one checkpoint item added before a known high-friction lecture segment.

## Primary Outcome

Segment rewatch rate delta two weeks after intervention.

## Secondary Outcome

Checkpoint correct-rate delta two weeks after intervention.

## Method

Preferred:

- switchback or cluster comparison where operationally feasible

Fallback:

- pre/post aggregate comparison with clear limitation note

## Data Minimization

The default analysis uses aggregate or pseudonymous course-level signals. Identified learner-level data is not required for S01.

## Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
| --- | --- |
| Student surveillance concern | no identified risk list, student-facing support language only |
| Instructor overtrust | xAI card includes uncertainty and approval gate |
| Overclaiming effect | publish uncertainty and limitation note |
| Unequal support | fairness review before scaling beyond S01 |

## Pre-Registered Decision Rule

Retain the intervention if:

- segment rewatch rate moves in the expected direction
- checkpoint correct rate does not regress
- student/instructor feedback does not indicate confusion or perceived surveillance

Revise or roll back if:

- measurement is inconclusive
- confusion increases
- student-facing support copy is challenged and upheld through Meiwaku feedback
